Escort Game Prototype (2024) (PC)

An asymmetrical Co-op platformer about safely escorting an NPC to the end of the level.

Engine/Tools: GameMaker, GML
My Role(s): Designer/Programmer
Team Size: 2
Duration: 2 days - Full time
Type: Pair Project


Description of project

This prototype was made by me and a friend. I was the designated Programmer while my friend was the artist. Design responsibilities were shared. The purpose of this project was to challenge ourselves through Rapid Game Prototyping of a Co-Op game where each player couldn't use the same input-units.

System: Split Screen

Why this mechanic exists

Enable both players to use the same monitor.

How it works

  • Two different viewports of the same game but with different controls.
  • Horizontal split.
  • Uses a "fake" black bar to divide the views by moving both viewports away from each other instead of edge-to-edge.

Expected outcome

  • Horizontal split gives the players better foresight for what's to come in the level compared to vertical.
  • Divider helps players differentiate what view is which.

Guard: Movement

Why this mechanic exists

To simply navigate the world.

How it works

  • The player can move, jump and wall jump by using the keyboard.
  • The player can move slightly in the air.
  • Jumping has Coyote time.

Expected outcome

The player feels in control of their own movement and not restricted by the game in any way.

Guard: Attack

Why this mechanic exists

To allow the player to eliminate any threats.

How it works

  • When the player presses the button, a shield appears for a brief time around them.
  • Any threat that makes contact with the shield will be eliminated.
  • Has a short cooldown between presses.

Expected outcome

  • The player uses the attack to protect the NPC they are escorting from any threats.
  • The player pays attention to only press the button when needed, rather than spamming.

Planner: Movement

Why this role exists

To simply navigate the world.

How it works

The player clicks and holds a button on the mouse to pan their view.

Expected outcome

The player doesn't feel restricted and move to the part of the level that they want without trouble.

Planner: Object manipulation

Why this mechanic exists

To allow creating paths for the NPC to move on.

How it works

The player clicks and drags a path and drops it at a highlighted spot in the level.

Expected outcome

The player controls the tempo of the game by deciding when it's time to move on, cooperating with the Guard.

NPC: Behaviour

Why this mechanic exists

Allow the NPC to move in a predictable way.

How it works

  • Will move in a direction until it reaches a wall.
  • If it reaches a wall, move in the opposite direction.
  • If it collides with an enemy or falls into a hoe, causes a Game Over.
  • If it collides with the goalpost at the end of the level, both players win.

Expected outcome

  • Becomes a ticking time bomb that forces the players to act.
  • Can be controlled by the players if they cooperate.

System: Enemy spawner

Why this mechanic exists

To provide pressure on the players.

How it works

  • When the NPC comes in contact with a hitbox, spawns enemies in a fixed pattern.
  • Will not spawn enemies more than once per hitbox
    • .

Expected outcome

  • The players strategize their approaches to each encounter.
  • The players don't feel pressured if they would backtrack to get some breathing room.

Design Goal

The limitations that we put on ourselves for the prototype was that it was going to be a Co-op game for two (2) players. The players could not share the same input-unit, meaning both could for example not use a keyboard as that would classify as the same input-unit.

The Objective

  • 2 Player Co-op only
  • Players cannot share an input-unit.
  • Cannot be played alone.

The Approach

  • Goal of the game: Escort the NPC from point A to Point B.
  • Each player has their own role (Planner or Guard)

Lessons learned

  • Behavior for moving up/down slopes in GameMaker is not automatically handled by the engine and has to be programmed on its own to work.
  • Don't use empty game space as a split-screen divider, as it will cause unintended behavior for the planner if the mouse would slip into it by accident, causing frustration.
